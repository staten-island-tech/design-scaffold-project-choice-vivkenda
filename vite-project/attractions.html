    The Summer Palace

    The Summer Palace an imperial garden located in Beijing. It was first built in 1750, but was destroyed in the war of 1860. In 1886, it was restored back to its original foundations. UNESCO reported that the Summer Palace is "a masterpiece of Chinese landscape garden design". The Summer Place was used as a summer retreat for the Chinese Royal Family.

    Terracota Army

    The Terracota Army is a collection of teracotta sculputres that depicts the army of Qin Shihuang, the first emperor of China. In order to achieve immortality, he built himself a tomb, as well as a underground city that was guarded by a vast army that includes warriors, infantrymen, horses, chariots, and all sorts of armor and weaponry.

    Great Wall of China

    The Great Wall of China spans cross northern China and southern Mongolia with a length of about 13,000 miles. It consists of numerous walls that built over two millennia. The wall was first built during the third century to prevent incursions of barbarian nomads. The best known and preserved section of the wall was built during the Ming Dynasty.
