Chinese Culture

Over 3,500 Years of History

Chinese Culture is one of the world's oldest cultures, originating from thousands of years of rich and entensive history. Important components of Chinese culture includes festivals, ceramics, architecture, music, literature, martial arts, cuisine, visual arts, calligraphy, philosophy and religion.

Chinese culture is very diverse and rich and as it expands across a large geographical region. Its influence is felt throughout the Asian region and has impacted the world on a large scale through the arts, sciences, cuisine, production and trade. Chinese culture has undergone a rapid and intense transformation over the past 50 years and continues to adapt to the modern world.

Customs

Chinese New Year

Date: 1st day of the first month

Chinese New Year, or the Spring Festival (春节), is the most important celebration observed in China, with its cultural and historic significance. The festival celebrates the start of a new year and brings good luck and prosperity for the new one.

Dragon Boat Festival

Date: 5th day of the 5th month

Th Dragon Boat Festival, also known as Duanwu Jie (端午节) or Double Fifth Festival, is one of the four top traditional Chinese festivals. It is often celebrated with traditional customs including dragon boat racing, eating sticky rice dumplings (zongzi).

Mid-Autumn Festival

Date: 15th day of the 8th month

The Mid-Autumn Festival, also known as the Moon Festival (中秋节), is a time for family members and loved ones to gather and celebrate the full moon. It is celebrated with traditional activities including lantern lighting and eating mooncakes.

National Day

Date: October 1

Chinese National Day (国庆节) is a public holiday in China celebrated annaully to commemorate the formal proclamation of the establishment of the People's Republic of China. It is celebrated with flag raising ceronomies, massive military parades, and grand rallies, and fireworks displays.

Dongzhi Festival

Date: December 22

The Dongzhi Festival (冬至), or Winter Solistice Festival, has its roots in ancient Chinese culture. It celebrates the change of season into the warmer and lighter days of spring. It is a day for families to selebrate together witha hearty meal of traditional food including Tang Yuan(湯圓), and Jiaozi (Dumplings).

Chinese History

A trip down China's History.

2200-1600 BC

Xia (Hsia) Dynasty

The Xia Dynasty is the first dynasty in Chinese History, but was often considered by scholars to be a mythical period of time due to the lack of records to prove its existence.

1600-1045 BC

Shang Dynasty

The Shang Dynasty is he earliest ruling dynasty of China established in recorded history. The era is known as a Bronze age where early forms of writing and intellectual thinnking had started to develop.

1046-256 BC

Zhou Dynasty

The Zhou Dynasty was among the most culturally significant of the early Chinese dynasties and the longest lasting of any in China's history. Among the concepts developed by the Zhou was the Mandate of heaven, a belief in monarch rule that would influence Chinese politics for centureies afterwards

221-206 BC

Qin Dynasty

The Qin Dynasty was founded by Qin Shi Huang, the first emperor of a unified China. It was marked by a period of central rule and developments in Chinese infrastructure, coinage, and a uniform written language.

206 BC - 220 AD

Han Dynasty

The Han Dynasty was considered a golden age, due to great advancements in technology, trade, government, science, and culture. The well known Silk Road was formalized during the Han, and so was the invention of paper.

220-589 AD

Six Dynasty

The Six Dynasties Period, also known as the 3 Kindoms was known as a chatoic warring period marked by political and social unrest, but despite the chaos, great advances were made in medicine, astronomy, botany, and chemistry

589-618 AD

Sui Dynasty

The Sui Dynasty was a short-lived imperial dynasty. It reunified the country after 4 centures of fragmentation and reinstalled of rule of ethnic Han.

618-907 AD

Tang Dynasty

The Tang Dynasty is considered a golden age of Chinese arts and culture. It saw a flourishing of Chinese poetry and literature, as well as an expansion of China's international influence.

907-960 AD

Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms Period

The Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period was a prolonged era of political upheaval and division in 10th century imperial China.

960-1279 AD

Song Dynasty

The Song Dynasty lasted over 3 centuries and was marked by a period of sustained economic growth. The earliest paper money in the world was introducted, a permanent Chinese navy was established, movable type printing was invented and unprecedented development took place in architecture, science and technology.

1279-1368 AD

Yuan Dynasty

The Yuan Dynasty, founded by Mongol emperor Kublai Khan, was the first foreign dynaty to rule China. It ended more than 300 years of divison and reunified China. The Yuan Period also saw a flourishment of foreign trade and developments in math and economic prosperity.

1368-1644 AD AD

Ming Dynasty

During the reign of the Ming Dynasty, China's population almost doubled due to argicultural innovations, and developments were also seen in drama, literature, the rise of porcelain, and its trade expansion outside of China that established cultural ties with the West.

1644-1912 AD

Qing Dynasty

The Qing Dynasty was the final imperial dynasty in China, and was ruled by the Manchurians. During this period, there were many unrests and conflicts within the nation and with western countries. Some well-known conflicts include the Opium Wars with Britian, and the Taiping and Boxer Rebellion within China.

1912-1949

The Republic Period

Period of political and social unrest and rebellions after the fall of the Qing Dynasty. This is when the CHinese Civil war happened to establsih the Republic of China.

1949-Present

The People's Republic of China

The establishment of the People's Republic of China - the modern China that we know it by.
